# Lesson 1: Further Reading and Resources

## Canonical Papers

1. **"Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction"** (Sutton & Barto)
   - The definitive textbook; chapters 1-4 cover fundamentals comprehensively
   
2. **"Q-Learning" (Watkins & Dayan, 1992)**
   - The foundational algorithm; essential reading for understanding temporal difference learning

3. **"Asynchronous Methods for Deep Reinforcement Learning" (Mnih et al., 2016)**
   - Introduces A3C and threading approaches to deep RL

## Online Resources

- OpenAI Spinning Up: https://spinningup.openai.com/ - Excellent practical introduction
- Deep RL course by Sergey Levine: Covers fundamentals and deep RL methodically
- Berkeley CS288: Deep RL course materials freely available online

## Practical Implementation

- OpenAI Gym: Standard RL environments for development and testing
- PyTorch Reinforcement Learning: Tutorial series for implementing algorithms
- TensorFlow Agents: Framework for building RL systems

## Key Concepts to Review

- MDPs and Markov property: Understand when this assumption holds
- Convergence guarantees: Know when algorithms are guaranteed to converge
- Function approximation challenges: Understand limitations before using deep RL
